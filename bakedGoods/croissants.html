Croissants

By: Henrik

These croissants are light, flaky, and buttery, with a delicate texture that melts in your mouth. Perfect for breakfast or a midday snack, they are sure to impress your guests with their authentic French flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 70g granulated sugar
  • 10g salt
  • 25g fresh yeast
  • 250ml whole milk
  • 250g unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1 egg, beaten

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the flour, sugar, and salt. Crumble the yeast into the bowl and mix well.
  2. Heat the milk until it is lukewarm. Add the milk to the bowl and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 10 minutes, until it is smooth and elastic. Form the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rise for 1-2 hours, until it has doubled in size.
  4. Turn the risen dough out onto a lightly floured surface. Roll it out into a rectangle, about 1cm thick.
  5. Cut the butter into thin slices and arrange them over two-thirds of the rectangle, leaving a border around the edges.
  6. Fold the unbuttered third of the dough over the middle third. Fold the remaining third over the middle, as if you were folding a letter. Pinch the edges of the dough together to seal.
  7. Turn the dough 90 degrees and roll it out again into a rectangle. Fold the dough again in the same way as before.
  8. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  9. Preheat the oven to 200Â°C. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  10. Cut the chilled dough into triangles. Roll each triangle into a croissant shape and place it on the prepared baking sheet. Brush the croissants with beaten egg.
  11. Bake for 15-20 minutes, until the croissants are golden brown.
  12. Serve warm and enjoy!

Enjoy your fresh Croissants!
